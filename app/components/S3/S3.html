Behavioural Rehearsal Examples

  • Answering the phone confidently
    1. Identify the actions that you will need to do to be successful. You could get help if need be with this. This list could contain actions like
    2. When I hear the phone ring I will breathe deeply and stay ‘calm and cool’
    3. I will move slowly to the phone aware of my breathing, breathing deeply and I will wait until the 3rd ring before picking up the ‘phone
    4. I will sit down on the chair and relax a moment, my body posture will be relaxed and radiate self confidence
    5. I will speak slowly and say, “824 635. Who’s speaking please?” and smile’
  • Next comes the behavioural rehearsal
    1. Carry out the actions as planned by answering the phone as if for real
    2. Repeat the rehearsal a number of times and modify your actions, as you feel fit to make the answering go even better
    3. You can extend this rehearsal exercise to include thoughts and images besides only actions.
  • To practise the related social skills of answering the phone, you can ask colleagues or friends to phone.
  • Now you are ready for ‘live action’, you can use the lessons from these experiences to refine the first three steps to get even better at answering the phone.
  • Public speaking
    1. You may have a particular presentation that you want to deliver - so focus on that one.
    2. The behavioural rehearsal could be carried out in increasingly challenging circumstances, for example by rehearsing in front of no-one
    3. by yourself – using a mirror
    4. before a trusted friend or colleague
    5. before a few more people
    6. in the actual place of the talk.
  • At each rehearsal stage you note down
    1. what is working well and
    2. making you feel good.
    3. areas you still need to improve as they are making you feel uncomfortable
  • Behavioural rehearsal ought to give you some real positive and measurable benefits to your personal wellness which will
    1. encourage you to continue to do it
    2. apply it to more aspects of your life you may then find it a rewarding way to invest some of your scarce time.

    • List two opportunities where you can apply behavioural rehearsal in your life. One could be work related, and the other related to a family, social or leisure context.
