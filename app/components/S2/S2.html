What is behavioural rehearsal?

  • Behavioural rehearsal is the process of routinely rehearsing activities that you are about to, or will soon be called upon to, do. These are normally actions that will help you achieve a goal
  • Rehearsal can be part of a learning process but it also stands alone as preparation for carrying out an action that you have carried out before, albeit not always very successfully.
  • Remember that all actors spend a significant proportion of their time rehearsing their roles “ practice makes perfect” life roles are no different
  • Behavioural rehearsal is about:
    1. Physically going through all or part of the action (as opposed to imagining doing it)
    2. Becoming more ready from doing it
    3. Building readiness and confidence that you can do it when you need to
    4. Doing it – well!
  • Rehearsing more prior to doing do things will mean you rely less on using ‘avoidance coping methods’ when life presents you with a challenge.
  • You can apply behavioural rehearsal in many ways, both in and outside of work, rehearsal could help you to:
    1. make telephone calls to gain information
    2. say “No” to extra or unreasonable demands
    3. overcome the fear of public speaking
    4. improve your performance in one of your leisure activities e.g. playing golf or singing in a choir..

How you can learn behavioural rehearsal

  • You need to identify the action(s) that you want to carry out; it helps to know the goal that you want to reach.
  • Decide the sub actions you will do and in which order.
    1. what,
    2. how, and
    3. when
